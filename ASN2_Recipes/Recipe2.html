Recipe 2: Jollof Rice

A rich, spicy West African rice dish cooked in a flavorful tomato-pepper sauce.

Servings: 6 Calories: ~450 per serving Prep Time: 20 mins Cook Time: 45 mins

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1 cup tomato stew (blended tomatoes, peppers, onions)
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cups chicken or beef stock
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste
  • Mixed vegetables (optional)

Preparation

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water until the water runs almost clear and set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a pot, add the chopped onions and fry for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste and fry briefly, then add the tomato stew and cook until the sauce thickens and the raw taste reduces.
  4. Add the stock, curry, thyme, bay leaves, seasoning cubes, and salt; mix well and bring to a gentle boil.
  5. Pour in the rinsed rice, stir, cover the pot, reduce to low heat, and allow it to cook.
  6. Check occasionally; if needed, add a little more stock or water until the rice is soft and the liquid is absorbed.
  7. Stir in mixed vegetables (if using), cover, and steam for a few more minutes.
  8. Fluff with a fork and serve hot.
